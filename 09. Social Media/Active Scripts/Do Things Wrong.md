# North-Star
This is me being exasperated with a lesson that should have been simple, something I should have gotten YEARS ago, but I didn't. And now I'm passing that on.

# Script
- Hook: You gotta do things WRONG now to do them BETTER later
- LME: Let me explain %% it's friccin dumb that you have to say this every time%%
- Rising Action: Schools instilled/ingrained in us this pass-fail system, and we have all this anxiety about doing ANYTHING, like, oh, what if my post doesn't go viral, what if that girl rejects me
- Turning point: But here's the thing… actually making the mistakes and then THINKING about what you did wrong is the BEST way to learn. This is one of those lessons that's so simple, and everybody says it in their own way, like "perfect is the enemy of great" or "trial and error." 
- Climax: And those are true, but the thing we've become so used to overlooking is the post-mortem. The willingness to do something wrong comes with a willingness to reflect on WHAT you did wrong, not so you feel bad about yourself or whatever, but so you can do it better next time.
- Closer: if you think about it, even being totally unwilling to make mistakes is a mistake that you can learn from.
- CTA: I don't have a CTA. Go do things wrong, especially if you're a perfectionist.
